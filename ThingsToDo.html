Seadoos, Surfing, Paddle Board, Helicopter Tours

Main Beaches:  *Makena Big and Little Beach, Kaanapali, Kapalua, Lahina, Kihea *Makena Beach is bikeable (mountain bike recommended) from the Wailea area. Makes for a fun adventure but be careful on the main road because there are parts with little shoulder.

Bikes/Mopeds:  Both bicycles and scooters/motorcycles are available, several vendors are located on Maui. Sunrise bike ride down from the volcano, Haleakala… anyone?

Snorkeling:  Various vendors have hour, day and weekly rates (rates will vary). Ah’Hikinu reserve and Maluaka beach have been mentioned as favorite snorkeling locations.  Various half-day trips are available from Ma’alaea to Molokini beginning early in the AM, including breakfast.  These can also include a visit to Turtle Town where you swim with the sea turtlles.

Pacific Whale Foundation’s cruises are more family friendly Website is www.pacificwhale.org.

Mentionable Golf Courses:  Kapalua (888-708-9705), Makena (808-891-4000), Wailea (888-328-MAUI)

 Other courses: Pukilani (windy), Elleair and Kaliki

           Club Rental:  www.mauiclubrentals.com  offers sets of clubs, delivered and picked-up…steel $25, graphite $30 /day (weekly rates available)

          Tee Times:  Stand By Golf 808-665-0800 or 808-875-8406 is an excellent resource for tee times (owned by the same guy, Dick Lowe, that runs the golf club rental service). Call after 5PM to get reduced pricing for next  day golf.

Tennis:  Public courts are available in Kihei, across the street from the Starbucks. Private courts and lessons in Wailea.

Horseback Riding:  Into Haleakala crater has been recommended

Walking:  Don’t miss out on a jog or walk on the Wailea path. This path will connect you to the main Wailea hotels, including the Four Seasons where the big day is!

Hiking:  Lots available, a recommended one in Lao valley, Kulu lodge, Kulu botanical Garden and Tedeshi Vineyard
